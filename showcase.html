Business Showcase — Selected Projects

Project A — UX Redesign for Campus Cafe (Group)

Role: Frontend lead & UI prototyper

Challenge: The cafe’s online menu and ordering process were confusing, which led to lots of phone orders and mistakes.

My Approach: I helped design a clickable Figma prototype and translated it into a responsive HTML/CSS prototype. I focused on simplifying the user journey and making the menu easy to navigate.

Outcome: Testing with stakeholders showed fewer order mistakes and a clearer, smoother online ordering experience, helping the cafe reduce phone workload.

Project B — Accessibility Improvements (Individual)

Role: Accessibility auditor & implementer

Challenge: A university webpage was difficult to use for some users due to low contrast and missing ARIA roles.

My Approach: I audited the page, then improved contrast, keyboard navigation, and added ARIA labels. I also documented the changes so others could continue improving accessibility.

Outcome: The page passed basic WCAG checks, making it more usable and inclusive for all users.

Project C — Problem-Solving Page Design Concept (Restaurant Case Study)

Role: Acting CTO & lead designer

Challenge: A local restaurant had no online presence for bookings or customer engagement. They relied on walk-ins, which made managing reservations and feedback tricky.

My Approach: I designed a prototype page that included social links, contact info, and a simple booking/feedback form that generates an email (simulated). I focused on a clean layout and intuitive flow so visitors could easily understand the restaurant’s offerings and take action.

Outcome: The prototype gave the restaurant a clear, practical starting point that could be handed to developers for production, improving potential engagement and bookings.

Design Concept & Why

The page follows a simple, linear flow: situation → value proposition → booking form → contact & socials. This makes it easy for visitors to understand the business, take action, and verify contact info. Forms are lightweight and client-side validated to match the brief’s "no live database" requirement while still producing an actionable email for the restaurant.
