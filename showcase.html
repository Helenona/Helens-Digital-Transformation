Business Showcase — Selected Projects

Project A — UX Redesign for Campus Cafe (Group)

Role: Frontend lead & UI prototyper.

Problem: The cafe's online menu and ordering flow were unclear, causing high phone orders and frequent mistakes.

Outcome: Produced a clickable prototype (Figma) and a responsive HTML/CSS prototype. Stakeholder testing showed reduced order confusion in user tests and a clear path to reduce phone workload.

Project B — Accessibility Improvements (Individual)

Role: Accessibility auditor & implementer.

Problem: A university web page had low contrast and missing ARIA roles, making it difficult for some users.

Outcome: Delivered an annotated report and updated HTML/CSS demonstrating improved contrast, keyboard navigation, and ARIA labels — validated against basic WCAG checks.

Project C — Problem-Solving Page Design Concept (Restaurant Case Study)

Role: Acting CTO & lead designer for the case study.

Problem: A local restaurant lacked an online presence for bookings + customer engagement; they received a lot of walk-ins but had no streamlined booking or feedback channel.

Outcome: Created a prototype Problem-Solving Page that integrates social links, contact details, and a booking/feedback form which generates an email to the restaurant manager (simulated). This served as a minimum viable feature set that could be handed to developers for production.

Design concept & why

The Problem-Solving Page uses a simple, linear layout: situation → value proposition → booking form → contact & socials. This order reduces friction: visitors immediately understand the business goal, can take action (book/feedback) and then verify contact/social proof. Forms are lightweight and validated on the client side to match the brief's "no live database" requirement while still producing an actionable email for the restaurant.
