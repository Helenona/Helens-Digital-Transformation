Business Showcase — Selected Projects

A collection of early-stage digital projects, prototypes, and redesigns I created during university and independent learning. Each project focuses on solving real business or user experience challenges through simple, thoughtful design.

Project A — UX Redesign for Campus Café

Role: Frontend Lead & UI Prototyper

Challenge: The café’s online menu and ordering flow confused students, causing heavy phone orders and frequent mistakes.

My Approach: I designed a clearer, more intuitive menu layout in Figma and created a responsive HTML/CSS prototype. I simplified the user journey by reducing clicks, improving visual clarity, and making common items easy to find.

Outcome: User testing showed improved navigation, fewer errors, and a smoother ordering experience, reducing staff workload and phone queries.

Project B — Accessibility Improvements (Individual)

Role: Accessibility Auditor & Implementer

Challenge: A university webpage had low contrast, unclear navigation, and missing ARIA roles, making it difficult for screen reader and keyboard users.

My Approach: I carried out an accessibility audit, improved text contrast, added semantic structure, labelled interactive elements, and enhanced keyboard navigation.

Outcome: The updated page passed key WCAG checks and became more inclusive and user-friendly for students with accessibility needs.

Project C — Restaurant Problem-Solving Case Study

Role: Acting CTO & Lead Designer

Challenge: A local restaurant lacked any structured online system for bookings, feedback, or digital customer engagement.

My Approach: I built a lightweight prototype page including a booking/feedback form that generates an email preview, social media integration, and clear business information. The design stays simple, intuitive, and user-centred.

Outcome: The prototype provides a strong starting foundation the restaurant could hand off to developers and begin improving customer engagement online.

Design Concept & Reasoning

The layout follows a clear flow: problem → value → action. Forms are kept lightweight (no live backend required for this coursework) but still practical with automatic email generation. This reflects realistic business needs while fitting within the assessment brief.
