Restaurant Digital Transformation Case Study

A beginner-friendly digital transformation project where I designed an online booking and customer engagement flow for a small independent restaurant. The goal was to understand a business problem, break it down, and create a simple prototype that provides real value.

Business Context

For this case study, I acted as a junior designer/strategist for a small local restaurant aiming to modernise their customer engagement. They lacked an online booking system, had no structured method for gathering feedback, and minimal social visibility.

My Problem-Solving Approach

I broke the project down into clear, manageable stages so I could focus on one step at a time:

  • Research: Reviewed small restaurant websites to identify common user journeys and features.
  • Planning: Mapped out a simple flow for table bookings and feedback submission.
  • Design: Created a clean, intuitive wireframe in Figma focusing on clarity and ease of use.
  • Build: Implemented the prototype using HTML and CSS with a lightweight structure.
  • Testing: Checked responsiveness, readability, and user flow on both desktop and mobile.

What I Learned

This project helped me build confidence in turning a real business challenge into a clear digital solution. I learned how to organise my ideas, test my assumptions, and create a simple user experience that still feels meaningful and practical.

  • Breaking down a business problem into small actionable steps
  • Designing simple user flows for bookings and feedback
  • Improving responsive layouts and accessibility awareness
  • Prototyping ideas quickly using beginner-friendly tools

Even though this was a beginner project, it gave me valuable real-world practice and strengthened my approach to digital problem-solving.
