Restaurant Digital Transformation — Problem-Solving

Business Context

For this case study, I acted as a designer and problem solver for a small local restaurant looking to modernize customer engagement. The goal was to create a simple online booking system, collect customer feedback, and make the restaurant more visible on social media.

My Approach

I approached the problem step by step, focusing on creating a prototype that was simple, easy to use, and realistic for a beginner-level project:

  • Research: Looked at similar small restaurant websites to understand common features and layouts.
  • Planning: Sketched a basic flow for online booking and feedback to keep it lightweight.
  • Design: Created a prototype page in Figma with clear navigation and a simple booking form.
  • Implementation: Used HTML and CSS to build a static version of the page, focusing on clarity and responsive design.
  • Testing: Checked the flow on desktop and mobile to ensure it was easy to use and accessible.

What I Learned

This project helped me practice practical problem-solving in web design, including:

  • Breaking a larger business challenge into small, manageable steps
  • Designing clear user flows for booking and feedback
  • Applying basic responsive design and accessibility principles
  • Prototyping ideas quickly and iterating based on simple testing

Even though this was a beginner-level project, it gave me confidence in tackling real-world problems and planning digital solutions.
