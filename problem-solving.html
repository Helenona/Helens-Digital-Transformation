Restaurant Digital Transformation Case Study

Oak & Ember is a small independent restaurant exploring how simple digital tools can improve the way they connect with customers. This case study looks at how modernising communication channels and making information easier to access can make life smoother for both guests and staff.

Business Context

The restaurant faced common challenges: customers struggled to get in touch during busy times, online information was scattered across social platforms, and bookings often came through inconsistent phone calls. My goal was to identify ways to simplify these processes and make the restaurant more approachable online.

Digital Solution Approach

To make a real difference, I focused on clarity, accessibility, and ease of use. Key steps included:

  • Designing a clean, responsive webpage that clearly explains services and offerings.
  • Making restaurant contact details easy to find and consistently presented.
  • Linking to all active social media platforms so customers could follow or reach out easily.
  • Designing a system (implemented on the Contact page) to automatically generate emails for bookings or feedback, without needing a database.

Outcome & Reflection

The digital updates create a much smoother experience for customers: finding information is straightforward, reaching the restaurant is simple, and sending structured enquiries takes just a few clicks.

Even small digital improvements can have a big impact — reducing stress for staff, helping customers feel heard, and building trust in the restaurant’s brand.
